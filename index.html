The Estrogen-Cortisol Metabolic Switch Explained:

When estrogen drops after age 40, your body compensates by producing more cortisol — the stress hormone. Elevated cortisol sends a direct signal to fat cells: "Store energy around vital organs for protection." This is visceral fat, and it's metabolically different from subcutaneous fat (the kind you can pinch).

At the same time, dropping estrogen slows your metabolism by 20-30%. Your gut microbiome shifts, producing fewer short-chain fatty acids that regulate fat storage. Your insulin sensitivity decreases, making every carb you eat more likely to be stored as belly fat.

This isn't a calorie problem. It's a metabolic signaling problem.
